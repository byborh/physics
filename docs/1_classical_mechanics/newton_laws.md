## üß† What Are Newton‚Äôs Laws?

Newton‚Äôs three laws explain how and why every object moves the way it does.

### ‚öñÔ∏è **First Law ‚Äì The Law of Inertia**

> An object remains at rest, or moves in a straight line at constant velocity, unless acted upon by an external force.

Mathematically:
$$

$$

**Meaning:**
If there‚Äôs no net force, nothing changes in the object‚Äôs motion.

**Example:**

Once it‚Äôs rolling, it keeps going ‚Äî unless friction or a wall stops it.

### üí• **Second Law ‚Äì The Law of Acceleration**

> The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force applied and inversely proportional to its mass.

Mathematically:
$$
\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = m \vec{a}
$$

Where:

* ( \vec{F}_{\text{net}} ): Net external force
* ( m ): Mass (in kilograms)
* ( \vec{a} ): Acceleration (in m/s¬≤)

**Meaning:**
The stronger the force, the greater the acceleration ‚Äî but heavier objects need more force to accelerate the same amount.

**Example:**
Pushing an empty shopping cart is easy.
Pushing a full one with the same speed requires more effort ‚Äî because it has more mass.

---

### üîÅ **Third Law ‚Äì The Law of Action and Reaction**

> For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Mathematically:
$$
\vec{F}*{AB} = -\vec{F}*{BA}

**Meaning:**
Whenever one object applies a force on another, the second object pushes back with equal force in the opposite direction.

**Example:**
When a swimmer pushes water backward with their hands, the water pushes them forward.
That‚Äôs why they move through the pool.

In short, they‚Äôre the **universal rules of motion** ‚Äî for everything that moves or resists motion.
